## About Me

My research bridges artificial intelligence, machine learning, and systems biology to develop computational methods for understanding cellular behavior in health and disease.

I have led and co-authored multiple peer-reviewed publications spanning bioinformatics, machine learning, and cancer biology. I earned my BS/MS in Bioinformatics through Virginia Commonwealth University's accelerated program in 2023 and stayed at VCU for my PhD in Integrative Life Sciences, under the supervision of Dr. Preetam Ghosh. I also had the privilege of joining Merck Research Laboratories as an AI/ML Co-op on the Methods and Modeling team in 2025, where I contributed to deep learning models for in silico gene perturbation and multimodal learning frameworks for genomic data analysis.

I am passionate about applying computational techniques to solve challenging biological problems and developing open-source tools that are both robust and reproducible. I also advocate for rigorous benchmarking to ensure computational methods are evaluated fairly and meaningfully.
